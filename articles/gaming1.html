Will Persona 4 Get a Remake?
Here's Everything We Know

Since its inception in 1996, ATLUS’ Persona series has steadily grown into a major global RPG franchise. With major successes like Persona 5 Royal and the recently released Persona 3 Reload, fans are now wondering: is a remake of Persona 4 next in line?

A Hint Toward Something Bigger: The “p4re.jp” Domain

What started as community speculation may now have some substance. On March 20, 2025, prominent Persona content creator ScrambledFaz revealed that a domain name, “p4re.jp” had been registered. While this could easily be dismissed as a hoax, there's precedent to support the theory.

In fact, prior to the official announcement of Persona 3 Reload, ATLUS registered the domain p3re.jp months in advance. If history repeats itself, this could mean an official Persona 4 Remake reveal within the next few months.

The Community Reacts

News of the domain quickly went viral, with the topic gaining over 6 million views on X (Twitter). Most reactions were overwhelmingly positive, with fans excited by the idea of Persona 4 receiving updated visuals using ATLUS’ modern game engine, reimagined gameplay features, fresh arrangements of the original soundtrack, and the return of its beloved cast.

Yet, despite the hype, the discussion has also sparked notable debate within the fanbase.

Why Some Fans Are Against a Persona 4 Remake

While many welcome the idea, others feel a Persona 4 Remake is unnecessary, at least for now. Some of the key reasons include:

1. Personas 1 & 2 Are Still Missing

Despite the success of modern titles, Persona 1 and Persona 2 remain largely inaccessible, having not seen a proper re-release or remake for over two decades. Fans argue that these foundational titles deserve a modern revival before Persona 4 receives another iteration.

2. Where Is Persona 6?

With Persona 5 having launched in Japan back in 2016, fans are growing impatient for the next mainline entry. By this point, the wait for Persona 6 has surpassed the gap between Persona 4 and 5, and there has been no official announcement from ATLUS. For some, the idea of a remake instead of moving the franchise forward feels like a step backward.

A Look Back: The Rise of Persona

To understand the significance of a Persona 4 Remake, it helps to look at the series’ journey:

  • 1996: Revelations: Persona debuts on the PlayStation. While not a global hit, it laid the groundwork.
  • 1999: Persona 2: Innocent Sin and Eternal Punishment release to solid reception in Japan.
  • 2006: Persona 3 launches, redefining the franchise with a darker tone, time management mechanics, and social simulation elements. It saved ATLUS during a financially difficult period.
  • 2008: Persona 4 is released, later followed by Persona 4 Golden in 2012. It became a fan favorite, praised for its characters, music, and emotional narrative.
  • 2017: Persona 5 hits the global market, bringing massive mainstream success. It spawned multiple spin-offs, collaborations, and elevated ATLUS to a new level of recognition.

Given this legacy, a Persona 4 Remake could be seen as a celebration of one of the series’ most iconic chapters—especially as Persona’s 30th anniversary in 2026 approaches.

ATLUS’ Recent Momentum

Despite mixed feelings about a potential remake, ATLUS has had an incredible year in 2024:

  • Persona 3 Reload – A modern reimagining of the classic, receiving critical acclaim.
  • Shin Megami Tensei V: Vengeance – A definitive version of the acclaimed SMT title.
  • Metaphor: ReFantazio – A bold new IP that exceeded expectations and showcased ATLUS’ storytelling prowess.

These successes indicate that ATLUS is riding a strong wave of momentum, setting the stage for an exciting 2025 and a landmark 2026.

What Could ATLUS Announce Next?

With Persona 4 Remake likely aiming for a 2026 release, fans are left wondering what else ATLUS could unveil for the franchise's 30th anniversary. If history is any guide, the 25th anniversary included Persona 5 Royal and Persona 5 Strikers, then multiple major announcements are possible.

Here’s what fans are hoping for:

  • An official Persona 6 announcement
  • Remakes or remasters of Persona 1 & 2
  • New spin-offs or multimedia projects (anime, mobile games, etc.)
  • A global Persona concert tour or anniversary event

Final Thoughts

While nothing is officially confirmed, all signs are pointing to something big on the horizon for Persona 4, most likely an announcement on June 6th at this year’s Summer Games Fest. Whether you're excited about a remake or holding out for Persona 6, it’s clear that ATLUS is gearing up for a monumental moment in the series’ history. And with 2026 marking three decades of Persona, the future of the franchise has never looked more exciting—or more uncertain.

So, the real question isn’t just “Will Persona 4 get a remake?”—it’s “What will ATLUS do next?”
