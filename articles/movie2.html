Most Streamed TV Shows of 2025 So Far

A list of the Top 10 Highly Streamed Shows of 2025!


As we progress through 2025, several television series have emerged as the frontrunners in streaming popularity. From gripping new dramas to beloved classics making a resurgence, here's a look at the most-streamed TV shows captivating audiences this year:




1. Adolescence

(available on Netflix)

Debuting on March 13, 2025, Adolescence has quickly become a cultural phenomenon. The British crime drama centers on 13-year-old Jamie Miller, who is arrested for the murder of a classmate. Notably, each of its four episodes was shot in a single continuous take, adding to its critical acclaim. In its first three weeks, the series amassed 96.7 million views on Netflix, securing its place as one of the platform's most-watched shows to date.




2. Severance

(available on Apple TV+)

Premiering on January 17, 2025, the second season of Severance continues to captivate audiences with its gripping narrative and innovative storytelling. The Apple TV+ workplace thriller delves deeper into the unsettling world of Lumon Industries, where employees undergo a procedure that separates their work and personal memories. Directed by Ben Stiller and featuring an ensemble cast led by Adam Scott, Patricia Arquette, and John Turturro, the 10-episode season explores the dire consequences of tampering with the severance barrier. With episodes releasing weekly, the season has already garnered widespread acclaim, solidifying its status as one of Apple TV+'s most anticipated releases.




3. The White Lotus

(available on Max formally known as HBO Max)

Debuting on February 16, 2025, the third season of The White Lotus has solidified its reputation as a masterclass in dark comedy-drama. Set against the lush backdrop of Thailand, the HBO anthology series explores themes of spirituality and existential reflection through the lives of wealthy guests and staff at a wellness resort. With its eight episodes airing weekly, the season has garnered widespread acclaim for its atmospheric storytelling and layered characters. In its first two months, the show has captivated millions of viewers, further cementing its status as one of HBO's most celebrated series.




4. The Last of Us

(available on Max formally known as HBO Max)

Debuting on April 13, 2025, the second season of The Last of Us has quickly become a cultural phenomenon. The HBO post-apocalyptic drama picks up five years after the events of the first season, following Joel and Ellie as they navigate a world even more dangerous and unpredictable. Notably, the seven-episode season introduces new characters and explores deeper emotional conflicts, earning widespread acclaim. In its first two weeks, the series has drawn millions of viewers, solidifying its place as one of HBO's most celebrated adaptations.




5. Black Mirror

(available on Netflix)

Debuting on April 10, 2025, the seventh season of Black Mirror has reignited its status as a cultural touchstone. The Netflix sci-fi anthology returns to its roots with six standalone episodes, exploring the eerie and unsettling consequences of modern technology. Notably, the season features a sequel to the fan-favorite episode "USS Callister," alongside fresh narratives that delve into themes of virtual reality, artificial intelligence, and dystopian futures. In its first two weeks, the season has captivated millions of viewers, reaffirming Black Mirror as one of Netflix's most iconic series




6. The Studio

(available on Apple TV+)

Debuting on March 26, 2025, The Studio has quickly become a standout in satirical comedy. The Apple TV+ series, created by Seth Rogen and Evan Goldberg, follows Matt Remick, the newly appointed head of Continental Studios, as he navigates the chaotic intersection of art and commerce in Hollywood. Notably, the show features an ensemble cast including Catherine O'Hara, Kathryn Hahn, and Chase Sui Wonders, alongside guest appearances by Hollywood icons like Martin Scorsese and Olivia Wilde. In its first three weeks, The Studio has garnered widespread acclaim and millions of views, solidifying its place as one of Apple TV+'s most talked-about releases.




7. Good American Family

(available on Hulu & Disney+)

Debuting on March 19, 2025, Good American Family has quickly become a gripping addition to Hulu's lineup. The drama series, inspired by the real-life case of Natalia Grace, follows the harrowing journey of a Midwestern couple who adopt a young girl with dwarfism, only to question her age and identity. Notably, the eight-episode series is told from multiple perspectives, exploring themes of bias, trauma, and public scrutiny. In its first three weeks, Good American Family has garnered widespread acclaim and millions of views, solidifying its place as one of Hulu's most compelling original releases.




8. Hacks

(available on Max formally known as HBO Max)

Debuting on April 10, 2025, the fourth season of Hacks has quickly become a standout in the world of comedy-drama. The Emmy-winning HBO series continues to follow the tumultuous yet endearing partnership between legendary stand-up comedian Deborah Vance and her sharp-witted writer Ava Daniels. This season, the duo navigates the high-stakes world of late-night television as they attempt to launch Deborah's new talk show. With its signature blend of biting humor and heartfelt moments, the 10-episode season has garnered critical acclaim. In its first two weeks, Hacks has drawn millions of viewers, reaffirming its place as one of HBO's most beloved series.




9. The Pitt

(available on Max formally known as HBO Max)

Debuting on January 9, 2025, The Pitt has quickly become a groundbreaking addition to HBO's lineup. The medical drama, set in the emergency room of Pittsburgh Trauma Medical Hospital, unfolds in real time over the course of a single 15-hour shift. Each of its 15 episodes captures an hour of the day, immersing viewers in the intense challenges faced by healthcare workers. Praised for its realism and emotional depth, the series has garnered widespread acclaim and averaged over 10 million viewers per episode in its first three months, solidifying its place as one of HBO's most-watched shows this year.




10. The Gardener

(available on Netflix)

Debuting on April 11, 2025, The Gardener has quickly become a standout in Netflix's international lineup. The Spanish-language romantic thriller follows Elmer, a young man emotionally numbed by a childhood accident, who works as a hitman under the control of his domineering mother, La China Jurado. Their gardening business serves as a front for their contract killings, but Elmer's life takes a dramatic turn when he falls in love with one of his targets.
